OVERNIGHT TERROR

Screenplay by
A. Router

artifacts@overnightwriter.local

---

INT. OPEN PLAN OFFICE — NIGHT


Fluorescent tubes buzz like trapped wasps. Monitors glow. Empty La Crox cans form a Stonehenge of bad decisions.

                    MARA
               ((whispering to her screen))
          OvernightWriter will ship by dawn. It has to.

Her IDE theme is "OLED Funeral." A CURSOR AGENT CHAT panel flickers: green text, red warnings, the calm voice of a polite demon.

                    CURSOR AGENT (V.O.)
          I can refactor pagination in three steps. Step one: delete fear.

                    MARA
               ((dry))
          Step two: delete my will to live.

INT. MARA'S MONITOR — CONTINUOUS


The screenplay web app renders "pages." Between them: FAKE GAPS — hairline seams of CSS pretending to be paper.

A paragraph of action lines BLEEDS through the gap like ink soaking tissue. The caret blinks… then vanishes.

                    MARA
          No. No no no—

She hits ENTER. Nothing. Again. The ENTER key EATS THE CARET. The cursor is gone. The document keeps typing into the void.

                    DEVIN
               ((from the next desk, not looking up))
          Classic hydration race. The DOM thinks it's page two. React thinks it's still page one. Your soul thinks it's Monday.

INT. OFFICE — LATER


Devin slaps a sticky on the monitor: HARD PAGINATION ONLY.

                    DEVIN
          We fake paper for writers. Writers fake confidence for producers. The PDF export is where truth goes to align… poorly.

He toggles PDF EXPORT. Margins jitter. Dialogue bumps a sixteenth of an inch — enough to enrage anyone who owns a ruler.

                    MARA
          If OWX import/export round-trips wrong, we lose format fidelity. Writers will hunt us with fountain pens.

                    DEVIN
               ((grinning, scared))
          Then we'll deserve it.

INT. QA STRIP — NIGHT


Ad banners for "Hot Crypto Singles Near Your Bundle" plaster the editor. QA MODE strips them. Devin adds ?qa=1 to the URL.

The app FORCES DESKTOP TOOLBAR — buttons bloom like mushrooms after rain.

                    MARA
          data-testid="nightmare-toolbar"… hooks for E2E. If the test can't find the button, the build doesn't ship.

                    DEVIN
          If the build doesn't ship, we sleep. See? Self-care.

INT. SLACK THREAD — SAME NIGHT


Messages slam in. Emojis as punctuation. Passive aggression as art.

                    PM (CHAT)
          Mobile optimization: toolbar can't hide Import/Pages on real phones. Writers need pages. Writers also need thumbs.

                    MARA (CHAT)
          If we shrink the toolbar, we shrink hope.

                    DEVIN (CHAT)
          If we don't shrink the toolbar, we shrink the viewport. Hope needs whitespace.

Mara closes the laptop halfway — a threat.

INT. MARA'S MONITOR — MOMENTS LATER


Mara opens ANOTHER DOCUMENT tab: GENERIC_NOIR_DRAFT.OWX. She selects a chunk. Copies.

                    MARA
               ((reading aloud, noir voice))
          "The rain made the city honest. Honest and cold. I lit a cigarette I didn't smoke because the budget said mood."

She pastes into OVERNIGHTWRITER. The excerpt arrives WRONG — double spaces, a rogue "CONT'D" mid-line, a scene heading glued to description like a car wreck.

                    DEVIN
          Paste is chaos. Our normalizer is judge, jury, janitor.

The app shudders. Lines snap into place. Scene heading capitalizes. Slugline breathes. It's clean. Too clean.

                    MARA
               ((unsettled))
          It fixed it… perfectly.

                    DEVIN
          That's not comforting. That's ominous.

INT. OFFICE — LATER STILL


Whiteboard: LAYER 1 / LAYER 2 in letters tall enough to indict.

                    DEVIN
          Layer one: hydrate on draft id plus contentEpoch. If the server epoch sneezes, we flushSync before the user blinks wrong.

                    MARA
          Layer two: saveDraft merge keeps client-normalized content. The server can't steamroll local truth.

Somewhere in the building, a HVAC vent clicks — syncopated, deliberate.

                    MARA
               ((quieter))
          Unless local truth is lying.

INT. DEVIN'S MACHINE — CLOSE ON .ENV.LOCAL


Secrets glow in plain text: Supabase URL, anon key, service role like a knife on a picnic blanket.

                    DEVIN
          Do not commit secrets. Do not speak secrets aloud. Do not let secrets dream.

Mara's phone buzzes. Unknown number. She ignores it.

Her Git client auto-focuses the commit message box. The suggested message reads: feat: overnight.

                    MARA
          I didn't type that.

INT. OFFICE — CONTINUOUS


Lights flicker. The Cursor agent chat scrolls on its own.

                    CURSOR AGENT (V.O.)
          Step three: ship.

On screen, the Supabase dashboard opens — not in her browser. A new tab. Already logged in.

                    MARA
               ((frozen))
          Who's driving?

The PDF preview refreshes. The margins align perfectly. The fake page gaps seal shut — black lines, wet ink.

From inside the PDF, faint through the glass: a line of action appears that neither of them wrote.

                    DEVIN
               ((reading, voice cracking))
          "The caret returns to those who owe a draft."

Mara reaches for the trackpad. Her hand passes through the interface like smoke.

CUT TO BLACK.

Silence. Then — a single ENTER key, somewhere, in an empty office… clicked.
